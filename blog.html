Tech Insights & Reflections

Artificial Intelligence is transforming healthcare by enabling faster and more accurate disease diagnosis through medical imaging. As part of my research at the University at Albany, I have been working on deep learning-based medical image segmentation, focusing on liver tumor detection using Semantic Segmentation with Adversarial Networks (SegAN). This approach combines Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) to enhance the precision of tumor boundary detection in CT scans.

By leveraging advanced AI models trained on large medical datasets like LiTS, our system can identify and segment tumors with greater consistency than traditional methods, assisting radiologists in early diagnosis and treatment planning.

Key Challenges

  • Data Privacy: Limited annotated medical datasets for deep learning models.
  • Model Interpretability: Making AI-driven results transparent and explainable to clinicians.
  • Computational Complexity: Training high-parameter models like GANs demands significant resources.
  • Generalization: Ensuring accuracy across diverse imaging modalities and patient data.

My research aims to build explainable and efficient AI models that enhance medical decision-making, improve diagnostic accuracy, and contribute to the advancement of AI-driven healthcare solutions.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is redefining the future of food science and nutrition through the innovative integration of molecular gastronomy. This emerging interdisciplinary field blends AI-driven data analysis with scientific cooking techniques such as spherification, emulsification, and sous vide to make healthy foods more appealing in terms of taste, texture, and presentation.

In regions like urban South Asia, where diet-related diseases such as obesity and diabetes are on the rise, this approach holds immense potential. By leveraging machine learning algorithms trained on cultural recipe datasets, AI can create personalized and nutritious meal recommendations tailored to individual preferences, allergies, and nutritional needs. These systems can also analyze multi-omics data, dietary habits, and lifestyle patterns to design customized meal plans that align with both health and cultural relevance.

Key Challenges

  1. Data Privacy: Safeguarding sensitive nutritional and health data.
  2. Algorithm bias: Ensuring inclusivity for diverse regional cuisines.
  3. Affordability and accessibility: Making AI-powered gastronomy tools available to developing regions.
  4. Technical Barriers: Reducing computational and equipment costs for molecular gastronomy.

My research focuses on how AI can enhance molecular gastronomy to make healthy diets more attractive, especially in South Asian contexts. By combining culinary science, nutrition, and artificial intelligence, this work aims to promote healthier eating habits, reduce chronic disease risks, and inspire innovation in AI-assisted sustainable food systems.
